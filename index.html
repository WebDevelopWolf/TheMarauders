  • 2016

    The Marauders-
    Origins

    Book 1

    Alexandria, or Lex as she's known to her friends, cannot believe her luck, university. She has finally made it. Yet destiny and two mysterious women calling themselves Black and Francis have other ideas for her and before she knows it, Lex is swept away in world very different to the one she knows, because Lex is a Marauder. One of four woman who are able to channel the earth's elemental energy and use it to have power and control over water.

    Meanwhile, across the country Seth, a research and development scientist, is dealing with the loss of his wife to a very aggressive cancer and being cohered into a diversion in his research by creatures seeking revenge on humans since 1666, The Pigeon Mafia.

    Now the Marauders are thrown into turmoil as one of their quartet is evolving, Seth is plotting against them and a mysterious discovery uncovers the existence of four birds of prey similar to themselves who can control the elements in tune with them. Will Seth and the mafia find them before the Marauders can and in her first real trial can Lex survive the stress of having the weight of the world on her shoulders?

  • 2017

    The Marauders–
    Black Ocean

    Book 2

    As punishment for her mistake in Milton Keynes, Lex is shipped off to Pigeon Point, California to become a temporary lighthouse keeper. Without her fellow Marauders, Lex finds Pigeon Point a bland, lifeless town, but then she meets her, Autumn. Lex battles with her emotions as she questions her sexuality and morals at having an affair with a married woman.

    The two soon find themselves in love and just as Autumn plans to leave her loveless relationship she finds herself pregnant and Lex's fears are realised as she leaves her. After one night of drinking alone, Lex is horrified to wake up the next morning and find that Autumn is dead and it is her fault. Lex struggles, lifelessly through the court case and pleading guilty intentionally ruins her life with the view to end it.

    Meanwhile, Josephine explores her own relationship with Gerry back home and it seems as though she has the perfect, passionate relationship, but soon discovers Gerry is not all he seems. And when Jane recalls the troubles she had with past love, The Marauders soon start to question how long it is they can hold on to happiness?

  • 2018

    The Marauders-
    Re-Alignment

    Book 3

    After the event in Pigeon Point, MI rewards the Marauders with a well-deserved holiday, camping in the Forest of Dean. A two-week getaway where they can relax and be themselves. At least that was the plan. An ear piercing siren cries out through the woods and once it stops, everything falls silent as the grave. Animals disappear, birds no longer sing and the Marauders find themselves herded by strange clicking and grinding sounds. Their only option is keep moving, but falling behind, it is not long before Lex disappears, snatched by subterranean humans known only as Grinders. Creatures that feed on elemental energy, the same energy controlled by the four.

    Can the other three Marauders find Lex in time? Or will there be nothing left of her after being painfully and brutally tortured by the Grinders? And can the group ever forgive MI for putting them up to a trial that may well kill one of their own? Priorities shift and friction between the Marauders and MI becomes inevitable as time for Lex is running out.

About the Author

Liane Stevenson is the author of the gripping 'Marauder' chronicles. She was first published as a poet at 19 years old and again as an author with 'The Marauders' when she was 22 years old.

She started writing as a poet in 2006 and was first published as a competition winner with her poem 'Wings to Fly', a sonnet about creativity and writing. When navigating the stresses of a third year Internet Technology university degree The Marauders started life as a joke between her and her friends for them to all escape the stresses of university together.

The Marauders, however, was much more popular than Liane expected and soon there was a demand for her to write the whole novel and once involved in Lex's world, Liane found it almost impossible to leave. Even when working as a web developer and volunteering as a Zoo Keeper, she found the time to tell Lex's story.

This year Liane plans to officially re-launch the first book in the 'Marauder' chronicles on Amazon Kindle whilst working on the second instalment, 'Black Ocean'.

Saving the world

One bird at a time

Liane, in her spare time, works as a volunteer Zoo Keeper at Gentleshaw Wildlife Centre in Eccleshall, UK. Gentleshaw is a registered charity who offer a sanctuary for exotic animals and birds of prey as well as being a small hospital for the rescue and recovery of wild birds of prey. The centre is now and always has been family-run, founded by Rob Smith in 1993, but now run by Rob's eldest daughter, Jenny Morgan, who worked at her father's side for many years. The centre is now home to over 30 species of bird of prey, primate, reptile, invertebrate, cats, dogs and many more.

From the moment she started working at the centre, Liane was swept away by the passion devoted by the owners, directors, volunteers and friends of the centre and devoted as much of her free time as possible to working alongside them. Much like Alexandria, she turned her hand to falconry and started to develop a special bond with the birds of prey at the centre. Moved by the indomitable spirit of the staff, Liane made the decision to pledge all profits from 'The Marauders' novels to Gentleshaw Wildlife Centre.

Each hard copy and digital copy of the book sold goes toward keeping these animals fed, warm in winter, cool in summer and cared for to the highest standard. These majestic birds of prey inspired Liane and captured her heart when writing about the elemental birds in the novel, now she wishes to give something back.
